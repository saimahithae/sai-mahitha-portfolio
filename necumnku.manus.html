About Me

I am a Data Analytics Engineering graduate student at Northeastern University with expertise in data mining, machine learning, business intelligence, and data engineering. My passion lies in transforming complex datasets into actionable insights that drive strategic decision-making.

Professional Journey

My journey in the world of data began during my undergraduate studies in Electrical and Electronics Engineering at Mahindra École Centrale. While working on various engineering projects, I discovered my passion for extracting meaningful patterns from data and using them to solve real-world problems.

At Infosys, I honed my technical skills by developing cross-platform web applications and integrating APIs. This experience taught me the importance of clean code, efficient algorithms, and robust testing methodologies – all crucial elements in the data analytics pipeline.

Currently, as a Research Assistant at Northeastern University, I'm contributing to cutting-edge neurotechnology research, developing a vision-testing instrument prototype using EEG data and machine learning techniques. This role has deepened my understanding of how data analytics can be applied to complex scientific challenges.

Professional Philosophy

I believe that data is only as valuable as the insights it provides. My approach combines technical expertise with business acumen to ensure that data analysis translates into tangible outcomes. Whether it's optimizing processes, identifying market opportunities, or predicting future trends, I focus on delivering results that matter.
